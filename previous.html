10/13/21

Datawrapping on deadline

You are part of a data reporting team in a newsroom! Your reporter colleague has just sent you a story and a dataset and they need you to make a quick chart displaying one of four findings. The story is here, and the dataset is here.

In your teams, make copies of the dataset. Start by trying to recreate the pivot table(s) and calculation(s) that got to the finding. Then, brainstorm what kind of chart would be best to display this finding. You should use FT Visual Vocabulary [https://ft-interactive.github.io/visual-vocabulary/] to choose a chart type. Think about what trend the finding is trying to describe and what kind of chart would best show that trend, not just the number that is the actual finding.

When you have a sketch, work together to get through the steps in Datawrapper to create a chart. You should use a color palette generator [https://coolors.co/] to choose your colors, and you should think intentionally about your design. Use annotations and highlights where possible to emphasize certain data points or trends.

When you are done with your chart, hit the publish button and copy the embed code. Paste your embed code into this document.

9/29/21

Data diary legibility

  1. Using the dataset you’ve chosen, choose two questions you want to ‘ask.’ They should be more complex than simple sums, but simple enough that you can use Sheets tools or pivot tables to find the answers. (Example: For a dataset on COVID numbers in New York State, including Syracuse, 'How many total cases were there in Syracuse today?' would be too simple, but 'What is the percent change of COVID cases from this time last year to now in Syracuse?' or 'What is the COVID case rate in Syracuse compared to the state of New York?' would be good.)
  2. Work on answering the two questions in your Sheet (keep your raw data untouched!). You must use three Sheets tool functions or math functions. You must produce a thorough data diary in a separate tab. Clearly label your findings, your data diary, your raw data.
  3. Share your Sheet with your assigned partner. Follow your partners’ data diary and see if you can reproduce their findings. If you have suggestions on how to make their directions clearer, or if you think they got to one of their findings in a flawed way, use the comment function in Sheets to give them feedback.
  4. Discuss your sheet with your partner and use the feedback they gave you to improve your data diary.
