9/15/21

Dataset vetting

You are a newsroom data reporter. Your editor has found a dataset that they are thinking about suggesting to another reporter for a story. They need you to vet the dataset and report back ASAP.

1. Download the attached document from Blackboard and name it ‘datasetname_lastname_lastname’ using the name of the dataset and the last names of you and your partner..

2. Answer the questions in this dataset memo to help your editor understand this dataset better.

3. Either you or your partner should place your document into the ‘shared_with_students’ GDrive folder.

4. When you present to the class, tell us whether or not you recommend your editor use this dataset, and your reasons for your decisions.

homework assignment

due on 9/18/21 at 11:59PM

To consume:

Listen to the Data & Humanity podcast with Mimi Onuoha and Lam Thuy Vo. Come prepared to discuss this episode at length on 9/20.

To do:

Choose an existing data set online and write a one-page paper on why it would be considered a good data source for a news story. This doesn't have to be a government data source, but it should be a dataset you are interested in. Explain the source of the data, the reason it was collected and potential limitations it may have. Include at least one story idea for this dataset. Some questions you could address in this paper: How was this data collected? Why is the method of data collection trustworthy and sound? What are the limitations of this data? Who could you talk to about this data? In what ways is this data valuable? What stories could you pursue with this dataset?
Papers should by Times New Roman font, size 12, with normal margins and borders. Please make sure your name and "Data literacy assignment #1" is at the top of the paper, and please make sure the title of the paper is the name of the dataset. Please include a link to the dataset at the top of the paper.
